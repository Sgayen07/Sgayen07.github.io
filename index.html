About Me

I am a PhD researcher specializing in Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, with a solid foundation in Statistics (M.Sc.). My work sits at the intersection of data-driven discovery and complex biological systems.

I am deeply passionate about the underlying mechanics of Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, and Generative AI. I believe that the future of biological research lies in applying the same mathematical rigor used in high-dimensional statistics to the development of robust, interpretable, and scalable AI models. I am actively exploring how LLMs and generative architectures can be adapted to solve challenging problems in bioinformatics, bridging the gap between theoretical statistics and practical machine learning applications.
